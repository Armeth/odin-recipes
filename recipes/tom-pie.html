Tomato Flower Pie

Description

This pie is considered a delicacy in the forest nation of Gridania. It is often served with sliced tomatoes on top and decorated with carefully peeled tomato skins shaped in the form of a flower to better create the illusion of a beautiful flower garden.

Enjoyed by members of the botanists guild on many a festive occasion this delicious pie is said to have the flavor of a magherita pizza (whatever that might be)

Ingredients

  • 2lbs medium tomatoes
  • 2tsp ground black pepper
  • 2tsp kosher salt
  • 1oz onion, thinly sliced
  • 4oz creme cheese, softened
  • 3 strawberries, finely chopped (optional)
  • 4oz swiss or mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 8 basil leaves
  1. Using a small knife, carfully cut the peel off each tomato in one long piece and roll them into rosettes.
  2. Slice the tomatoes, arrange in a single layer on a baking sheet, and season with salt and pepper.Bake at 400 degrees F for 20 minutes.
  3. Seperately mix together the creme cheese and strawberries.
  4. Assemble pie by alternating layers of cheese, tomatoes, basil, onions, and creme cheese in a prepared crust. Finish with extra cheese on top.
  5. Steam the mussels until all shells are open.
  6. Bake uncovered for about 40 minutes and garnish the top with remaining basil and the tomato rosettes.
